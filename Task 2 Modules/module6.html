Module Intent

This module focuses on correct shutdown, storage, and end-of-shift standards that keep the workshop safe.

Core idea: Leave the workspace safe, clean, and ready for the next user.

Assessment Focus (Task 2)

Shutdown discipline

Leave machines isolated and stable before cleaning or storage.

Safe storage

Return tools and consumables to correct, labeled locations.

Housekeeping control

Remove swarf, waste, and trip hazards at end of task.

Handover readiness

Leave the area safe and usable for the next student/team.

Safe Shutdown Sequence

1
Stop operation

Turn off the tool and wait until all moving parts fully stop.

2
Isolate power

Unplug or isolate before any cleaning or adjustments.

3
Clean and inspect

Remove swarf/dust and check for faults or damage.

4
Store correctly

Coil cords loosely and return tools/PPE to correct locations.

Why Shutdown and Storage Matter

Safety

Prevents trips, slips, accidental start-ups, and contact injuries.

Tool life

Reduces preventable damage and extends equipment serviceability.

Productivity

Ensures the next user can begin safely and efficiently.

Storage Rules

Hand tools

Clean, dry, and store in labeled locations.

Power tools

Coil cords loosely, protect cutting components, and empty dust bags.

Consumables

Store by type/size in dry, labeled containers.

Never acceptable

Do not hang tools by cords or store tools in wet areas.

End-of-Shift Standards

Area condition

Benches wiped down and machines left safe.

Equipment status

Tools returned correctly and faults tagged/reported.

Waste and PPE

Floors clear, waste disposed, and PPE cleaned/stored.

Records

Update job card or required handover notes.

Knowledge Check: Multiple Choice

1) What is the safest first step after finishing a machine operation?

Isolate and confirm all movement has stopped.

2) Why should tools never be stored in wet areas?

Moisture can cause corrosion and electrical hazards.

3) What is the best way to leave power leads for storage?

Loose coils without kinks.

4) Which housekeeping action best reduces slips and trips?

Clear waste and keep walkways unobstructed.

5) Why is end-of-shift reporting important?

It transfers fault and completion information to the next user.

6) A tool is hot after use. Correct storage action is to:

Allow safe cool-down before final storage.

Knowledge Check: Scenario Responses

Scenario 1: Why must tools be cleaned and put away immediately after use?

It prevents damage, removes hazards, and ensures the next user can work safely.

Scenario 2: Why should cords not be wrapped tightly around a drill or grinder?

Tight wraps can break conductors and insulation, creating faults and shock risk.

Scenario 3: Name three tasks students must complete before leaving the workshop.

Isolate and clean tools, clear waste/floors, and store equipment/PPE in correct locations.

Scenario 4: Why must a tool cool down before storing it?

Hot components can burn users and damage storage materials or nearby equipment.

Scenario 5: You find swarf under a machine after cleanup. What is the correct response?

Remove it immediately and recheck the area so the handover standard is actually met.

Scenario 6: Why should faults be reported even if the tool is already put away?

Unreported faults can be missed and expose the next user to preventable hazards.
