Module Intent

This module builds the planning habits required to choose the right tool, follow instructions, and confirm requirements before any work starts.

Core idea: Know the job, plan the job, choose the right tool, then work safely.

Key Knowledge and Skills

Read first, cut second

Start with the job card, drawing, and SOP so you know the expected outcome.

Match tool to material

Select tools based on material, thickness, and the finish/tolerance required.

Use the latest version

Check revision numbers so you are not working from outdated instructions.

Plan the safe sequence

Mark, clamp, cut, deburr, inspect, and clean in the right order.

Stop and clarify

If anything is unclear or unsafe, stop and confirm before continuing.

Understand the Job Before Selecting Tools

Use this 4-part check to pick the right tool without overload.

  1. Goal: What must the part do or look like when finished?
  2. Material: What is it made from and how thick is it?
  3. Method: Which tool gives the required finish and accuracy?
  4. Safety: What control is non-negotiable before you start?
Quick check: If you cannot say goal + material + tool + control in one sentence, stop and ask.

Reading and Interpreting Job Instructions

Focus on what each source is for and the action it tells you to take.

Job card / Gantt

Look for

Sequence, timing, priority.

Action

Plan the order of steps and when to start.

Drawing / sketch

Look for

Dimensions, tolerances, symbols.

Action

Pick tools that meet the accuracy and finish.

SOP / supervisor

Look for

Safe method, PPE, updates.

Action

Confirm the method before touching the tool.

Right Tool Workflow

1
Confirm the job

Read the job card, drawing, and SOP so you know the target.

2
Identify the material

Note type, thickness, and finish/tolerance.

3
Select the tool

Choose the tool that suits the task and material.

4
Set controls

Plan PPE, guarding, and clamping before starting.

5
Check and start

Clarify any uncertainty, then begin the job.

Minimum Information Checklist

Material type and thickness/diameter.
Required finish and tolerance.
Operation type (cut, drill, shape, deburr).
Latest job card/drawing revision.
Relevant SOP and JSA controls.

Version Control Quick Check

1
Check the version

Confirm the revision number and date of issue.

2
Check authorization

Look for who approved the change and the revision notes.

3
Confirm the change

Example: "Job Card - Revision C - Updated cutter size for hole".

Communication and Confirmation

Ask

Use clear questions when dimensions or requirements are unclear.

Confirm

Repeat instructions back to check understanding.

Report

Escalate hazards or documentation errors immediately.

Example question: "Can you confirm the hole size and tolerance before I drill?"

Stop Work Triggers

Instructions are unclear or conflicting.
Tool choice is uncertain or unsafe.
Materials are defective or incorrect.
Hazards are not controlled.
Documentation appears out of date.

Knowledge Check: Multiple Choice

1) Which document should you check to confirm tool and PPE requirements?

The tool SOP.

2) Why must you confirm the latest revision of a job card or drawing?

To avoid using outdated instructions.

3) What should you do if the drawing and job card conflict?

Stop and ask the supervisor.

4) Which step comes first in a safe work sequence?

Read and confirm instructions.

5) What should you do if you are unsure which tool is suitable?

Ask and confirm before starting.

Knowledge Check: Scenario Responses

Scenario 1: You need to cut a piece of 1.2 mm sheet metal. Which tool do you select and why?

Tin snips or metal shears - they allow clean cuts on thin sheet metals without overheating or distorting the material.

Scenario 2: The drawing shows a 10 mm hole must be drilled. What must you check before choosing a drill bit?

Check the material type (to choose the correct bit), check the drill bit size is accurate, and ensure the drill bit is undamaged.

Scenario 3: Why must you follow the tool SOP instead of using your own method?

To ensure safety, reduce mistakes, maintain quality, and meet workplace compliance requirements.

Scenario 4: Why could using an outdated document cause an unsafe situation?

The old instructions might specify the wrong tool, wrong material, or outdated safety steps.

Scenario 5: You are unsure about a dimension in the drawing. What should you do?

Stop work and ask the supervisor for clarification. Never guess or assume.

Scenario 6: Why must clamping be done before cutting or drilling?

To prevent movement, slipping, or tool kickback, ensuring accuracy and safety.

Scenario 7: What two factors affect which file a worker should choose?

The material (e.g., mild steel vs aluminium) and the amount of material to remove (coarse vs fine cut).

Scenario 8: You notice the drawing shows a bend, but the job card does not. What should you do?

Stop and check with a supervisor - never assume which document is correct.
