What This Module Teaches

This module prepares students for direct observation by following the same sequence as the assessor checklist.

  • Part A Planning: interpret requirements, select/setup machine and tools safely.
  • Part B Working Safely: follow SOP and WHS/PPE during operation.
  • Final check: compare machined output to plan and adjust if required.
Assessment alignment: Section 3 Part A (PC1-PC6) and Part B (PC7-PC9).

Step 3: Common Reasons Students Lose Marks

Weak pre-start briefing

Project requirements are incomplete or unclear when explained to teacher.

Setup gaps

Tool install, guarding, or material clamping steps are skipped or rushed.

Inconsistent safety behavior

SOP/PPE followed at start but not maintained throughout operation.

No corrective action

Out-of-spec components are identified but not adjusted/re-machined.

Knowledge Check: Multiple Choice

1) Which criteria set belongs to planning/setup phase?

PC1-PC6.

2) What must happen if machined output does not meet project specification?

Re-machine/adjust until specs are met.

3) Which behavior demonstrates SOP compliance during operation?

Following documented operating sequence and controls.

4) Why is verbal confirmation to the teacher part of observed criteria?

It demonstrates understanding of job requirements before action.

Knowledge Check: Scenario Responses

Scenario 1: You installed a cutting tool but did not check guard settings. Which criteria are now at risk?

Part A criteria for guard setup (PC5) and safe preparation are at risk, with flow-on WHS non-compliance during operation.

Scenario 2: During operation you remove earmuffs briefly to hear instructions. Why can this still affect assessment outcome?

Observed WHS/PPE compliance must be consistent throughout machining activity, not intermittent.

Scenario 3: A part is 2 mm out of required dimension after machining. What must you demonstrate next?

Measure against specification, identify non-conformance, and apply appropriate adjustment/re-machining until within required tolerance.

Scenario 4: What is a strong verbal pre-start statement that supports PC1 and PC3?

State project details, selected machine, selected tools, required process sequence, and key tolerance targets before setup begins.
