Module Intent

This module explains how students apply WHS duties in a manufacturing workshop and complete manual handling tasks without creating risks for themselves or others.

Core idea: Identify hazards early, control the risk, then complete the task using safe body mechanics and approved procedures.

Assessment Focus (Task 1)

Recognise hazards early

Identify hazards before starting and continue scanning during the task.

Apply the right controls

Use hierarchy of control, SOP requirements, and pre-start checks.

Handle materials safely

Use correct posture, handling aids, and team lifts to reduce injury risk.

Report and respond

Record incidents and near misses clearly so controls can be improved.

WHS Foundations in Plain Language

Concept What it means in practice
Duty of care Follow procedures, use PPE, report hazards, and do not put others at risk.
Hazard vs risk Hazard is the source of harm; risk is the likelihood and consequence.
Hierarchy of control Eliminate, substitute, isolate, engineer, administer, PPE (last line only).
Manual handling risk factors Force, awkward posture, repetition, vibration, and long duration.

Machine Safety and SOP Rules

Follow the SOP

The SOP defines machine-specific PPE and safe operating sequence.

Pre-start checks every time

Do not operate until checks are complete and controls are in place.

Respect tags and isolation

Out of Service means unsafe to use until cleared by supervisor/teacher.

Manage noise exposure

Tasks like grinding require hearing protection and awareness of exposure time.

Workshop PPE Examples

Eye Protection

Safety glasses protect against flying chips and dust during cutting, drilling, and grinding.

Hearing Protection

Ear muffs reduce the risk of hearing damage from grinders and other loud tools.

Hand Protection

Work gloves protect against cuts, abrasions, and hot material handling.

Foot Protection

Work boots protect against dropped material and sharp objects on the workshop floor.

Pre-Start WHS Checklist

PPE selected for the task and fitted correctly.
Machine guard and emergency controls checked.
Work area clear of trip and slip hazards.
Workpiece clamped or secured before operation.
Out of Service / unsafe tools reported immediately.
Supervisor/teacher informed of any uncertainty.

Safe Manual Handling Method

1
Plan the move

Check weight, shape, path, destination, and need for assistance.

2
Set controls

Clear obstructions and use trolley, wheelbarrow, or team lift where needed.

3
Lift with control

Feet stable, load close, neutral spine, knees bent, no jerking.

4
Move and place

Avoid twisting, keep vision clear, and set down in a stable position.

Manual Handling Essentials for Task 1 Evidence

Highest injury area

Back injuries are the most common outcome of poor manual handling.

Correct stance

Feet slightly apart, knees bent, and load held close to the body.

Equipment to reduce strain

Material trolley, wheelbarrow, and pallet jack where suitable.

Worker obligation

Use approved safe systems of work and required training procedures.

Manual Handling Equipment

Pallet Trolley

Reduces lift strain and improves load control.

Wheelbarrow

Useful for moving materials over short distances.

JSA and Risk Matrix Workflow

1
List hazards by task step

Break the job into steps and identify the hazard at each stage.

2
Rate pre-risk

Assess likelihood and consequence before controls are applied.

3
Apply controls

Use hierarchy of control, then confirm PPE and supervision needs.

4
Rate post-risk

Record residual risk and confirm controls are effective.

Typical Task Hazards and Controls

Task Step Main Hazard Key Controls
Cold saw / hacksaw cutting Contact with blade, unsecured workpiece Guard fitted, work clamped, hands clear, eye and hearing protection.
Measuring and marking Sharp points and edges Keep hands clear, stable surface, controlled marking technique.
Drilling / tapping Entanglement, heat, moving workpiece Use vice, secure clothing/hair, check tagged cord, allow cooling.
Stamping / filing / cleanup Flying particles, sharp edges, chemical contact Clamp work, use file handle, wear eye protection and gloves as required, follow SDS.

Emergency and Incident Response

1
Stop and isolate

Cease work for uncontrolled hazards and make area safe.

2
Escalate immediately

Notify supervisor/teacher and follow emergency procedures.

3
Treat and secure

First aid or emergency response, then isolate unsafe equipment.

4
Record evidence

Who, when, where, what happened, injury details, actions taken, and witnesses.

Knowledge Check: Multiple Choice

1) What is the best way to minimise a hazard?

Eliminate (remove) the hazard.

2) When should pre-operational checks be completed?

Each time, before the machine is used.

3) What does an "Out of Service" tag indicate?

The item is unsafe to operate and must not be used.

4) Grinding a number of welds for most of a shift: what hazard is most likely?

Excessive noise.

5) What PPE is required when using a pedestal drill?

Safety glasses, ear protection, and a workshop apron (all required).

6) Which body area is most likely to be injured when lifting a heavy load?

The back.

7) What is the correct stance to lift a load from the floor?

Feet slightly apart, knees bent, load close to the body.

8) Under manual handling regulations, employees must:

Make full use of the employer's safe systems of work.

9) Which item is an example of manual handling equipment?

All of the above.

Knowledge Check: Scenario Responses

Scenario 1: A student is cut while using an angle grinder. What key details must be recorded in the incident report?

Record who was injured, date/time, exact location, activity being performed, description of the incident and injury, immediate treatment, witnesses, and who the incident was reported to.

Scenario 2: You need to lift a heavy piece of steel from the floor. What steps do you take before and during the lift?

Assess weight and path, use a trolley or team lift if needed, keep the load close, feet slightly apart, knees bent, neutral spine, and lift smoothly without twisting.

Scenario 3: A machine guard is missing before use. What is the correct response?

Stop work, isolate or tag out the equipment according to site procedure, and report the issue immediately.

Scenario 4: You identify a sharp-edge hazard during a task. How do you record and control the risk in the JSA?

Record the hazard, assess likelihood and consequence (pre-risk), apply controls such as clamping work and wearing eye protection, then record the post-risk rating.
